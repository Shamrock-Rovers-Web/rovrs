Subject: rov.rs — Our new link shortener is live

Hi team,

We've built a branded link shortener for Shamrock Rovers at **rov.rs**. This lets us create short, trackable links for everything we do — social posts, email campaigns, QR codes on posters, matchday material, sponsor activity — all from one dashboard.

## What it does

- **Short links** — Turn long URLs into `rov.rs/tickets`, `rov.rs/vs-bohs`, `rov.rs/shop` etc.
- **Click tracking** — See how many clicks each link gets, broken down by day
- **QR codes** — Generate print-ready QR codes (PNG and PDF) for any link, with scan tracking built in
- **Campaign tracking** — Tag links with campaign and channel so you can see what's working

## How to use it

The admin dashboard is at **admin.rov.rs** (you'll log in through Cloudflare — use your usual work email).

### Creating a link
1. Go to **Create Link**
2. Enter a **slug** — this is the short word after rov.rs (e.g. `vs-bohs` gives you `rov.rs/vs-bohs`). Lowercase letters, numbers, and hyphens only.
3. Paste the **destination URL** — where people end up when they click or scan
4. That's it — everything else is optional. You can add a title, channel, campaign, sponsor, match details, expiry date etc. if you want, but you don't have to.
5. Hit **Create Link** — it's live immediately

### Copying your link
After you create a link you'll see the short URL and a **Copy** button. You can also:
- Go to **Links** at any time and hit **Copy** next to any link — this copies the full `rov.rs/...` URL to your clipboard
- From there you can paste it straight into Instagram, X, an email, or wherever you need it

### Quick create
If you just need a fast link with no extras, use **Quick Create** — slug, URL, done.

### QR codes
After creating a link, you can download a QR code right there. Or go to **QR Codes** in the menu to generate, download, and manage QR codes for any link. These come in screen and print sizes.

### Checking performance
The **Dashboard** shows total clicks, top links, and QR scan counts. For individual link stats, go to **Links** and check the click count column.

## A few tips

- **Keep slugs short and readable** — `rov.rs/tickets` is better than `rov.rs/spring-2026-ticket-promo`
- **Use the channel field** — it helps later when comparing Instagram vs email vs QR performance
- **QR codes on print material should use the QR Codes page** — those scans are tracked separately so you can measure print vs digital
- **Expired links** automatically redirect to the tickets page, so match-specific links won't point at old content forever

Let me know if you have any questions or if something doesn't work as expected.
